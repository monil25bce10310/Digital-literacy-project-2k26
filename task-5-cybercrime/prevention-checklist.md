# Stay Safe Online — Prevention Checklist

##  UPI & Financial Safety

1. **Never share your UPI PIN with anyone** — not even
        someone claiming to be a bank official or customer
        support representative. Banks never ask for your
        PIN over a call, ever.

2. **Scanning a QR code debits money, it does not credit it**
        — never scan a QR code sent by someone claiming
        they want to send you a payment. This is one of
        the most common UPI tricks used on students.


##  Links & Apps

3. **Never click on links sent by unknown numbers**
        on WhatsApp or SMS — these are often disguised
        as bank notifications, delivery updates, or prize
        messages but are actually phishing pages or
        location tracking links in disguise.

4. **Never install screen sharing apps like AnyDesk
        or TeamViewer on anyone's request** — once
        installed they get complete access to your phone
        including your UPI apps and banking details.


##  Calls & Impersonation

5. **Be suspicious if a caller says nothing and you
        end up saying "hello hello" repeatedly** — this
        could be a deliberate voice recording attempt.
        Criminals use these recordings to train AI models
        that clone your voice or your relative's voice
        to trick family members into sending money.


##  Device Security

6. **Enable biometric lock on all your UPI and banking
        apps** — fingerprint or face unlock ensures that
        even if your phone is stolen or someone gets
        physical access to it, they cannot open your
        payment apps.


##  If You Get Scammed — Act Immediately

7. **Report it immediately at cybercrime.gov.in or
        call helpline 1930 (available 24x7)** — the
        faster you report, the higher the chance of
        the transaction being traced and recovered.
        Do not wait or feel embarrassed to report.

8. **Call your bank immediately and request them
        to freeze the transaction** — many banks can
        reverse unauthorized transfers if they are
        reported within a few hours of the fraud.
        Keep your transaction ID and UPI ID of the
        fraudster ready as evidence.



*National Cyber Crime Portal — cybercrime.gov.in*
*Cyber Crime Helpline — 1930 (24x7)*
