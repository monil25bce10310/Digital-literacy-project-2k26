# Case Study — UPI/Online Payment Fraud
Realistic Fictional Scenario

## What is the Crime
UPI/Online Payment Fraud is a form of cybercrime where criminals manipulate victims into transferring money through digital payment platforms like Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm by using deception, impersonation, and psychological pressure.

## How it Typically Happens
The process usually begins with the criminal identifying a target through social media or leaked contact databases. They then call the victim while saying nothing on their end — waiting for the victim to repeatedly say "hello, hello" — which is secretly recorded and used to train an AI voice model that can convincingly replicate the victim's close relative's voice.
In the next step, the criminal calls the victim disguised as a known relative, claiming to be in an emergency situation and urgently requesting a UPI transfer. Alongside this, they send anonymous tracking links disguised as legitimate messages — such as fake delivery updates or bank notifications — to monitor the victim's location and gather more personal information without raising any suspicion.

## Who is Targeted
Farmers, daily wage workers, and less educated individuals are targeted most frequently as they are less familiar with digital fraud tactics. However even educated people fall into this trap when emotional pressure and urgency are involved.

## Consequences
Victims suffer immediate financial loss which is rarely recovered. Beyond money, many experience anxiety, loss of trust in digital platforms, and in some rural cases, devastating impact on their family's livelihood since their entire savings get wiped out in a single call.
