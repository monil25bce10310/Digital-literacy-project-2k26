# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

A simple checklist to help you use social media responsibly
and protect yourself online.

---

## ✅ DO's

1. Keep your profile set to **private** on personal accounts
         so only people you trust can see your content.

2. Think before you post — ask yourself if you would be
        comfortable with your parents, professors, or a future
        employer seeing it.

3. Use **different passwords** for different social media
        accounts and update them every few months.

4. Report and block accounts that harass you or make
        you uncomfortable — most platforms have an easy
        option for this.

5. Verify information before sharing it — spreading
        fake news or unverified content can seriously
        damage your reputation.

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

1. **Do not share your financial status online** — this
        includes salary, pocket money, expensive purchases,
        or anything money related. It attracts the wrong
        attention and can make others uncomfortable.

2. **Do not share personal problems publicly** — venting
        online about fights, family issues, or personal struggles
        might feel relieving in the moment but others can
        use it to judge or tease you later.

3. **Do not share your real-time location** — avoid posting
        stories or updates that reveal exactly where you are
        right now. It is a serious personal safety risk.

4. **Do not accept friend or follow requests from strangers**
        — not everyone online has good intentions, especially
        accounts with no posts, no followers, or a recently
        created profile.

5. **Do not post during conflicts or when you are angry**
        — emotional posts are hard to take back and almost
        always make situations worse.



*Remember — the internet never truly forgets.
What you post today can follow you for years.*
